Mental Health Disorders

Depression

A serious mood disorder that leads to persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and a loss of interest in activities once enjoyed. It can affect not only emotions but also cognitive functions, energy levels, and physical health. Without proper support, depression may interfere with daily life, work, and relationships.

Anxiety

A mental health condition marked by excessive worry, nervousness, and irrational fear about everyday situations. It can manifest through physical symptoms such as increased heart rate, restlessness, and difficulty concentrating. If left unmanaged, anxiety can disrupt a person’s personal and professional life, leading to avoidance behaviors and emotional distress.

Stress

A natural response to external pressures or demands, often triggered by work, personal relationships, or significant life events. While short-term stress can be motivating, chronic stress may lead to mental exhaustion, anxiety, and physical health issues such as high blood pressure and weakened immune function. Learning to manage stress effectively is essential for maintaining long-term well-being.

Bipolarism

Also known as bipolar disorder, this condition is characterized by extreme mood swings that alternate between periods of intense emotional highs (mania or hypomania) and deep depressive episodes. These shifts can impact energy levels, judgment, behavior, and relationships, making it essential for individuals to seek treatment and adopt coping strategies to maintain stability.

Eating Disorders

A group of serious mental health conditions, including anorexia nervosa, bulimia nervosa, and binge-eating disorder, that involve distorted body image and unhealthy eating behaviors. These disorders can lead to severe physical complications, emotional distress, and difficulties in daily functioning. Early intervention and professional support are crucial for recovery.
